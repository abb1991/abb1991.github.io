Hieronymus Bosch's painting The Garden of Earthly Delights is as transcendent as it is provocative. Bosch's use of bizarrly sexualized humans in uncommon to unearthly situations brings to mind a proto-Freudian system of sexuality, with the characters of his work being a product and summation of life's excesses and indulgances.
